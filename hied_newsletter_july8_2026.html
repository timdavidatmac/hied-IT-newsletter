Two Items Worth a Look Before Fall

macOS, iOS, and iPadOS 26.5.2

Apple released 26.5.2 updates for macOS, iOS, and iPadOS, along with Safari 26.5.2, on June 29. We'd encourage IT teams to review the release notes and deploy on your normal patch cycle. macOS release notes →  ·  iOS/iPadOS release notes →  ·  Safari release notes →

Office 2019 for Mac — Read-Only Starting July 13

Microsoft is ending support for Office 2019 on July 13, 2026. After that date, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook will open files in read-only mode. If your campus still has Office 2019 deployed, this is the last week to plan an upgrade path. Read more →

Keynote, Pages, and Numbers — Update to 15.1 and Later

The Apple Creator Studio versions of Keynote, Pages, and Numbers require a new universal version (15.1 or later) for Mac, which can coexist with 14.5 until you're ready to upgrade everyone. If you're not using the subscription features, you can suppress prompts to subscribe on behalf of your users with the suppressPrompts managed preference key (15.1+), and suppress the privacy disclosure screen with suppressAgreements (15.3+). Important: if users have password-protected documents, notify them to follow the steps in Apple's deployment guide before removing the 14.5 versions. Version 15.3, now available, adds direct Pixelmator Pro image editing for Creator Studio subscribers.

Volume Purchase Free Apps

Later this year, managing free apps using volume purchasing won't require acquiring and managing licenses in Apple School Manager and Apple Business. This simplifies the use of free apps for organizations and saves time for IT teams. When you add a free app to your organization, it becomes available for assignment to your users and devices with your device management service without requiring a quantity of licenses. If you already have licenses for an existing free app, you retain use of them in case the app later requires payment. All free apps you add after this change use the new behavior. Read more →

In the News

🎪 MacAdmins Conference — Happening Now at Penn State

The Mac admins community's flagship annual conference is underway this week at Penn State, running through Friday, July 10. If you couldn't make it out to State College, session recordings and slides are typically posted in the weeks following. View the schedule →  ·  Watch for recordings →

Jamf Pro 11.29.1 — Attended and Unattended Platform SSO

Jamf Pro now supports both Attended and Unattended Platform SSO enrollment flows. The Unattended flow allows PSSO registration to complete automatically during enrollment without user input — useful for lab and shared-device deployments. Release notes →

Total Economic Impact of Apple Mac At Work

A new Forrester Consulting study, released in June and commissioned by Apple, found $760 in TCO savings per Mac over five years, 100 minutes reclaimed per employee per month, and an 80% reduced likelihood of data breach via lost or stolen assets. Useful reference for procurement and lifecycle conversations. Read the study →

New Book for Apple Admins — Mastering Swift, Available for Pre-Order

Charles Edge, Jeremy Bannister, and Joel Rennich have a new book on Swift scripting for device automation, MDM integration, and enterprise systems — written specifically for client platform engineers and device administrators. Available for pre-order now; ships September 15. Pre-order →
