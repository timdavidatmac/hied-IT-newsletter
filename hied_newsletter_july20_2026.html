What's New

Apple to Increase Spend with Broadcom to Produce Billions More U.S. Chips

Apple announced a new multiyear commitment with Broadcom to design and produce custom silicon components and cutting-edge wireless connectivity technologies for a wide range of Apple products. The new agreement, expected to exceed $30 billion, will lead to the production of more than 15 billion U.S.-made chips and support hundreds of American jobs. Apple has been working with the administration and businesses across the U.S. to help create an end-to-end silicon supply chain in America, and today's announcement advances those efforts. Identity & Platform SSO

Platform SSO comes in several flavors, each with a different user experience and approach to identity security: Password Sync, Secure Enclave, and Smart Card, each with its own strict delineation of supported features. In a recent Mac Admins Podcast episode, Okta's Arki Krowczynski and Dan Hefley joined hosts Marcus Ransom and Selina Ali to discuss the current state of the PSSO landscape and how to figure out which method fits your use case.

If you're configuring PSSO with Jamf Pro, a few guides from HCS Technology Group are worth bookmarking. Their Okta Platform SSO Simplified Setup guide covers Okta FastPass, Okta's passwordless, phishing-resistant authentication built on Device Access certificates, with step-by-step configuration for Password and Secure Enclave setups. For institutions using Microsoft Entra ID, start with their Jamf Pro and Intune Company Portal PSSO integration guide, then layer on the Simplified Setup addendum, which covers a macOS 26 feature that pauses Setup Assistant for an identity provider sign-in before the first local account is created, so users land at the desktop already registered for Platform SSO with no separate post-login step required.

In the News

⚠️ New macOS Malware Campaigns — Stay on Your Normal Patch Cycle

Security researchers have reported multiple new malware campaigns targeting macOS in recent weeks, including techniques designed to trick users into running malicious commands and lock devices to pressure victims for credentials. As always, we won't detail attack mechanics here — the best defense is staying current on your normal update cycle and reminding staff and students to be cautious with unexpected prompts to run commands or install software from unfamiliar sources. U of Utah MacAdmins: A Deep Dive on macOS 27 from SAP's Mac Team

The University of Utah MacAdmins Meeting recently covered macOS 27, a real transition point for fleet planning with Intel Mac support ending and Rosetta 2 nearing retirement. Robert Hammen, Principal Mac Consultant at SAP, where he helps manage nearly 60,000 Macs and previously spent five years at SpaceX overseeing the Crew Tablet iPad program, walks through what's changing: expanded Declarative Device Management, new network and certificate management options, FileVault and Platform SSO improvements, Managed App Framework support, and practical guidance on testing macOS 27 before it ships using AppleSeed for IT. A substantial, well-worth-the-time session if you're responsible for fleet strategy this fall. Watch the full session (51 min) →

🔭 Worth a Look

Interested in Local AI Clusters?

Apple's latest Mac computers with Thunderbolt 5 have quietly become a serious platform for running large AI models locally, without cloud infrastructure. At WWDC26, Apple detailed JACCL, a new RDMA-over-Thunderbolt communication backend for MLX that dramatically speeds up distributed inference and training across multiple Macs — now official platform capability, not just a demo (MacRumors coverage).

exo is one tool putting this to practical use: it auto-discovers Macs on your network, splits models across their combined memory, and serves standard OpenAI/Claude/Ollama-compatible APIs — no manual cluster configuration required.

If your team is considering this, a few real-world details worth knowing before you try it, via Apple Silicon benchmarker Alex Ziskind's hands-on JACCL cluster writeup: RDMA has to be enabled per-machine in macOS Recovery, a full Thunderbolt mesh requires six cables for four nodes, and one missed environment variable can mean a 5–6x performance hit. Ziskind also found that a single higher-spec Mac sometimes outperforms a two-node cluster for certain workloads — worth testing before committing budget to a multi-Mac setup.

🌟 Accessibility Note

New research published in Autism in Adulthood estimates that between 135,400 and 286,254 autistic students are currently enrolled at U.S. colleges and universities, a substantially higher figure than prior estimates as low as 60,000. Researchers say the true number is likely still higher, since many students don't disclose or request accommodations.
