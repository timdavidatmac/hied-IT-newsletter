⚠️ Important Notice

Prepare Your Network for Stricter Security Requirements

Upcoming Apple OS releases will introduce stricter TLS network security requirements — devices may refuse connections to servers with outdated or non-compliant configurations, including those involved in MDM, enrollment, and software updates. Audit your environment now; remediation can take significant time, especially for third-party-managed servers.

In the News

Microsoft Company Portal Now Supports Platform SSO

Microsoft Company Portal 5.2604.0 now officially supports Apple Platform SSO with Simplified Setup for Microsoft Entra, bringing native macOS login with M365 credentials, Secure Enclave-based passwordless authentication, and automatic device registration — all built directly into the platform. For institutions already on Intune and Entra, this is a meaningful step toward streamlined Mac onboarding without additional third-party tooling.

FileVault Recovery Key Issue on macOS Tahoe

If you use the fdesetup command-line tool to enable FileVault, be aware that the personal recovery key may not be displayed on macOS Tahoe. Worth reviewing if this is part of your provisioning workflow.

Announcements

Education Store — UNiDAYS Verification Now Required

Apple's Education Store now requires UNiDAYS verification to access education pricing — online and in-store. Students, faculty, staff, and parents can verify their eligibility through UNiDAYS, and signing in with an Apple Account keeps you verified for a full year. Your campus community may encounter this when making personal education purchases.

Visit the Education Store →   |   UNiDAYS FAQ →

End-to-End Encrypted RCS Messaging Now in Beta

Apple and Google have rolled out end-to-end encrypted RCS messaging in beta, beginning with iOS 26.5 and the latest Google Messages. For campuses with mixed iPhone and Android populations, this brings a meaningful security upgrade to cross-platform messaging — encryption is on by default and indicated by a new lock icon in RCS conversations.

Read more →

ASBMUtil — Open-Source Tool for Apple School & Business Manager API

ASBMUtil is a new open-source Swift app and command-line tool that taps into the Apple School & Business Manager API, letting admins query device info, assign or unassign MDM servers, and resolve device-to-server assignments across their fleet. Worth a look for anyone managing large device deployments in ASM. (Community-developed — evaluate before deploying in production.)
