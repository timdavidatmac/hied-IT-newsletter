🌟 WWDC 2026

Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference Kicks Off Monday, June 8

Apple has unveiled an exciting lineup for its annual Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC), taking place June 8–12. The conference brings together developers from around the world to explore the tools, frameworks, and technologies that will empower them to create groundbreaking experiences across Apple platforms.

WWDC26 will kick off June 8 with the Keynote and Platforms State of the Union, introducing incredible updates for Apple platforms, including AI advancements and exciting new software and developer tools. Apple will also welcome more than 1,000 developers, designers, and students to celebrate in person at Apple Park on June 8.

Apple Keynote

Monday, June 8 · 1:00 p.m. EDT

WWDC will open with a first look at the latest updates coming to Apple platforms. The Keynote will be available to stream on apple.com, the Apple TV app, and the Apple YouTube channel. On-demand playback will be available after the conclusion of the stream.

Platforms State of the Union

Monday, June 8 · 4:00 p.m. EDT

Shortly after the Keynote, the Platforms State of the Union will take a deeper dive into exciting new features, APIs, and technologies that will further empower developers to create powerful experiences across Apple platforms. Available to stream on the Apple Developer app, website, and YouTube channel. On-demand playback will be available after the conclusion of the stream.

Video Sessions and Guides

With new sessions on tools, technologies, and design, WWDC helps developers learn directly from Apple engineers and designers. All sessions will be available on the Apple Developer app, website, and YouTube channel. Curated guides will also help developers navigate the week's biggest announcements by platform and topic.

⚠️ Important Notice

Prepare Your Network for Stricter Security Requirements

Upcoming Apple OS releases will introduce stricter TLS network security requirements — devices may refuse connections to servers with outdated or non-compliant configurations, including those involved in MDM, enrollment, and software updates. If your team hasn't audited your environment yet, now is the time — especially ahead of OS betas arriving with WWDC.
